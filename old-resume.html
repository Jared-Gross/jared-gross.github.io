"Write" Stuff


The “Write” Stuff professional development session, held at the Border Land School Division Office in Altona, built on the momentum of Day 1 at Ross L. Gray School and focused on helping educators strengthen their writing instruction practices. Drawing inspiration from Nancie Atwell’s Lessons That Change Writers and Carl Anderson’s How to Become a Better Writing Teacher, the session explored how to immerse students in meaningful writing experiences and help them learn from both published authors and peers. A strong focus was placed on inquiry-based, group learning, where educators collaborated to practice lesson plans and explore strategies through shared discussion and reflection. Using texts like My Five Senses, teachers connected literature to personal experience, modeling how to guide students in doing the same. The session emphasized the use of mentor texts to teach craft, conventions, and process, while also encouraging the use of student writing as mentor material. Teachers learned how to conduct whole-class and small-group immersion, guide students in reading like writers, and highlight the humanity of mentor authors. Participants were encouraged to gather stacks of high-quality texts and mine them for teachable moments, expanding their teaching repertoire through professional reading and thoughtful planning.
