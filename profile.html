Introduction

My journey into teaching was inspired by the many supportive and dedicated teachers I encountered throughout my education. Their guidance paved the way for me to pursue teaching as a career, and I believe I possess a unique and valuable skill set that can be used to educate and inspire the next generation. At the core of my teaching philosophy is the belief that education should be personalized. Teachers must take the time to listen and engage with students, whether in the classroom or even in passing moments like a brief conversation in the hallway. Building positive relationships is essential, as communication and social connection are lifelong skills that support success. In my classroom, I see myself as both an instructor and a mentor, fostering a learning environment that encourages curiosity, problem-solving, and open discussion. My goal is to teach high school mathematics, science, and humanities in a Hutterian community, while also integrating my passion for computer science and robotics to provide cross-curricular learning experiences. My structured and organized nature, combined with a problem-solving mindset from my programming background, allows me to approach teaching with clarity and purpose, ensuring my students are well-equipped for the future. 

Teach people, not subjects.

Conclusion

Since beginning my education degree, my perspective on teaching has evolved significantly. I have come to appreciate the vast array of resources available to educators and the many ways in which lessons can be tailored to meet students’ needs. A question that constantly guides my approach is: “What is the best way to teach this?” In the next five years, I aspire to be a professional, calm, confident, and consistent teacher. My goal is to provide students with a personalized academic experience while fostering critical and creative thinking skills. Above all, I want to create a classroom environment where students feel heard, challenged, and inspired to engage with the world around them.

Experience with Children and Adolescents

My understanding of working with young people was shaped by hands-on experience in the classroom even before starting my university studies. Spending a year assisting in various grades allowed me to explore all levels of education and confirm my passion for teaching. During my practicum at Brennan School, I focused on teaching Kindergarten under the guidance of my mentor, Elma Maendel, but I also gained experience working with students up to grade 9. Over time, I have worked with students across all grade levels, from Kindergarten to grade 12. Initially, my interest in education stemmed from the joy of teaching early learners, but as I progressed through my mathematics degree, my focus shifted towards high school education. My practicum experience reinforced this transition, as I found myself drawn to more advanced topics that allowed for deeper critical thinking and problem-solving.

Successes and Challenges in Practicum

One of my biggest takeaways from my practicum was developing my “teacher voice,” which has helped me establish a strong presence in the classroom. I also learned the importance of simply listening—taking a few seconds to engage with students in the hallway can make a significant difference in their day. My teaching strategies center on organization, consistency, and professionalism, which have proven effective. However, I faced challenges in my early teaching experiences, particularly in understanding school policies and structuring lessons to maximize engagement. One area I initially struggled with was closing lessons effectively, but with practice and feedback from my mentor, I have improved my ability to wrap up lessons in a meaningful way. My experience in a Kindergarten classroom provided me with hands-on learning in classroom management, literacy instruction, and student engagement strategies, which have helped shape my overall teaching approach.

Teaching Goals and Aspirations

Preferred Grade Levels and Subjects

I would prefer to teach grades 8 to 12, particularly in Computer Science, because of my strong background in the subject. I believe I can offer students in these grades a unique perspective on problem-solving and critical thinking, which are essential skills for their future success. These upper-grade levels are a critical time when students can benefit the most from this teaching style, as they are developing higher-level reasoning and analytical skills.

Incorporating Virtue Education

One meaningful experience from my early years of education was a weekly class on virtues taught by one of my teachers. This practice left a lasting impression on me, and I strongly advocate for integrating character education into the curriculum. If given the opportunity, I would like to implement a similar approach in my own classroom to help students develop moral reasoning and ethical decision-making.

Emotional Intelligence and Student Well-Being

An issue I have observed in many individuals is the mislabeling of emotions, which often leads to difficulty in managing them effectively. Emotions are a natural and important part of life, and students need guidance on how to properly identify and navigate their emotions. By fostering emotional intelligence in the classroom, I aim to help students develop self-awareness, emotional regulation, and healthy coping strategies, which are crucial for both their personal and academic growth.

Contributions Beyond the Classroom

Beyond academics, I envision myself contributing to the school's extracurricular activities by establishing a makerspace with resources for 3D printing, robotics, and electronics. My background in computer science enables me to engage students in innovative ways, such as programming activities connected to their coursework. I also see 3D printing and modeling as valuable tools for fostering creativity and problem-solving. By incorporating these interests into my teaching, I hope to create a dynamic learning environment where students feel encouraged to explore and apply their knowledge in meaningful ways.

Self-Awareness & Critical Reflection

I am mindful of the “curse of knowledge” and actively work to structure my lessons in a way that ensures all students, regardless of prior understanding, can engage with the material. I firmly believe that assessments should only evaluate concepts that have been explicitly taught, as testing students on unfamiliar material is neither fair nor productive. My background and experiences have shaped me into a professional and articulate educator, and I strive to model these qualities for my students. Since I plan to teach in a Hutterite community where many students already know me personally, my focus will be on helping them develop professional communication skills that will benefit them in both academic and real-world settings.

Areas for Growth

One of my key goals for improvement is refining how I close lessons to ensure students leave with a clear understanding of key concepts. I am also interested in exploring professional development opportunities that focus on effective teaching methods for specific topics, allowing me to enhance my instructional techniques. Attending workshops and staying engaged in professional learning communities will help me continue evolving as an educator. Looking ahead to my next practicum, my primary goal is to improve my classroom management skills, particularly in handling disruptions and guiding students more effectively.
