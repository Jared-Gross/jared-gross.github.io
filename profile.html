schoolTeaching Philosophy

Students learn best when they feel safe, respected, and capable.

My goal is to create a classroom where students understand expectations, feel comfortable asking questions, and are supported as they work through challenges. Strong planning matters, but so does the ability to adjust when students need more time, a different explanation, a visual model, or a hands-on activity.

I aim to make learning clear and accessible through modelling, questioning, examples, guided practice, formative checks, and individual or small-group support.

Structure and responsiveness

I value prepared lessons, clear routines, and organized learning sequences while remaining flexible enough to respond to student needs in the moment.

Confidence through clarity

My mathematics background helps me support students who feel discouraged by difficult concepts. I focus on patterns, steps, practice, and encouragement.

tips_and_updatesInstructional Practice

Clear outcomes, guided practice, and active learning.

My instructional approach focuses on clarity, structure, and student understanding. I design lessons that connect to curriculum outcomes and give students opportunities to activate prior knowledge, acquire new learning, and apply their understanding.

fact_checkAssessment and Evaluation

Assessment should guide learning, not just measure it.

I use assessment to understand where learning is strong and where more support is needed. Formative assessment helps me respond to students in real time and adjust instruction when needed.

Assessment practices

  • Pre-assessment of prior knowledge
  • Questioning during lessons
  • Observation during individual and group work
  • Exit tickets and closure questions
  • Feedback during guided and independent work

Assessment philosophy

Assessment should help students and teachers move forward. I value feedback, reflection, student check-ins, and practice tasks that connect clearly to learning outcomes.
